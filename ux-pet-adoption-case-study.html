Pet Adoption Case Study

Define

Goals

A company wants to create a digital animal adoption service. This service will allow its users to filter by their needs, saving time and allowing them to connect with animals more easily. Better matches and quicker adoptions will help reduce costs for animal shelters.

Research

Themes that appeared during the research phase were that:

  • Location was the most common search parameter
  • Photos were powerful
  • It was not always clear how to book an appointment
  • Meeting the animal was extremely important

"Would be nice to look at the website, but better to go in-person to meet a few. "

- Interviewee 1, potential adopter

"Keep an open mind. First impressions are really important."

- Interviewee 2, has adopted several dogs over the years

Analysis & Design

I created an affinity diagram and customer journey map during the analysis. These provided additional insights into the animal adoption Research had revealed that people felt both excitement as well as concerns about finding a good fit.

During the sketch phase, I tried to reduce the number of steps for arranging a meeting as much as possible.

Prototype & Testing

I chose to create a medium-fidelity prototype. It provided a close representation of the final product for testing while still having a fairly quick turnaround time.

I ran the first version of my prototype by the user first. He wasn't happy about having to create an account. The tester preferred the second version, which had a "Continue as Guest" option, since it presented fewer barriers.

Conclusion

I discovered that many of the existing animal adoption services do not have a smooth appointment setup experience. Since the user interviews revealed that meeting the animal was extremely important, I focused on reducing the barriers to setting up a meeting. I included:

  • A map of the shelter with a directions link
  • Short form with ability to fill out as guest
  • Option to simply call the shelter

Hopefully these elements will help more people find their new best friend as well as help reduce costs for the shelters by helping move animals through more quickly.

Retrospective

The prototype tester mentioned that he would be likely to fill out the form to meet an animal if quickly taking his lunch break in his work truck, but would place a phone call instead if he was at home and had more time. Since the user's location may have an impact on actions taken, it might be interesting to do testing in different settings.

A potential opportunity for improvement might be an additional focus on animals who are being fostered. Foster homes may be able to add behavioral insights that could help pet adopters make their decision.

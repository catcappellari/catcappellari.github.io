Pet Adoption Case Study

Define

Goals

A company wants to create a digital animal adoption service. This service will allow its users to filter by their needs, saving time and allowing them to connect with animals more easily. This will allow for more efficiency and reduced costs for volunteers and staff with animal agencies by creating better connections and moving animals into their forever homes more quickly.

Research

The research phase included a competitive benchmark, message board mining, and user interviews. The process moved from birds' eye view (benchmark) to a an individual level with the interviews. I found that there are already several digital pet adoption services. Making it as easy as possible to meet the animals seemed like a competitive advantage.

Competitive Benchmark

The competitive benchmarking process mapped the current pet adoption landscape. It revealed established norms and potential trouble spots. It yielded a few key findings:

  • Type of animal and location were the most common basic search components
  • Animal photos were used prominently
  • There were discrepancies on how easy it was to set up an appointment

Message Board Mining

Information on pet adoption was gathered from the Dogsey.com message board and Reddit. The information gathered there confirmed that the most common filters about location, size, and "gets along with" are all very popular. Two other ideas included asking the foster family about the animal's personality and the suggestion of making the application available online.

User Interviews

Insights were collected from two user interviews. The tester demographics were as follows:

  • Participant 1: potential adopter
  • Participant 2: has adopted several dogs over the years

Customer Journey Map

The customer journey map shows where the adoption website visit fits within the user's larger decision-making process. It also identifies various emotions throughout the adoption process. Top insights included:

  • Users rely heavily on photos of the animal
  • Visiting the animal is extremely important to making a decision
  • Shelter and next steps info is often incomplete or confusing

Sketches

Sketches allowed me to quickly iterate over solutions for the issues that surfaced during the research and analysis phases. I tried to reduce the number of steps for arranging a meeting as much as possible.

Prototype

I chose to create a medium-fidelity prototype. It provided a close representation of the final product for testing while still having a fairly quick turnaround time. I created two versions of the final steps and tested with one user. The first version had the shelter phone number and a log in/account creation form, while the second one included the shelter phone, a map of the shelter location, and an option to continue as a guest through the questionnaire form. The user preferred the second version since he didn't have to create an account and because the map drew him in.

Conclusion

During this project, I discovered that many of the existing animal adoption services do not have a smooth appointment setup experience. Since the user interviews revealed that meeting the animal was extremely important, I focused on reducing the barriers to setting up a meeting. I included:

  • A map of the shelter with a directions link
  • Short form with ability to fill out as guest
  • Option to simply call the shelter

Hopefully these elements will help more people find their new best friend as well as help reduce costs for the shelters by helping move animals through more quickly.
